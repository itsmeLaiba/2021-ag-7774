Glasses detection is an essential component in various computer vision applications, including face recognition, virtual try-on systems, and facial expression analysis. The project focuses on glasses detection in computer vision and its applications in various domains. It consists of two parts: identifying images with glasses and visually allocating glasses to the detected images. The first part utilizes Dlib's frontal face detector to locate faces and extract facial landmarks using the "faceShape.dat" model. The region of interest (ROI) around the eyes and nose bridge is extracted using these landmarks. Image processing techniques, such as Gaussian blur and Canny edge detection, are applied to the ROI to determine the presence or absence of glasses based on the presence of white pixels (edges)
